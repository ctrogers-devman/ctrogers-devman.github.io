The man who originiated the search for extraterestial life

Carl Sagan introduces a new Space documentary.

Here is a timeline of Dr. Sagan's life

  • 1934 - Born in Brooklyn, New York City
  • 1939 - Attends New York's World's Fair
  • 1951 - Graduates from Rahway High School in New Jersy
  • 1960 - Receives Doctorate from University of Chicago
  • 1960-1962 - Serves as the Miller Fellow at University of California Berkley
  • 1963-1968 - Assistant Professor at Harvard
  • 1968 - Denied acemdemic tenure at Harvard
  • 1968 - Associate Professor at Cornell University
  • 1970-1996 - Full Professor at Cornell University
  • 1970-1972 - Director of Laboratry for Planetary Studies at Cornell University
  • 1972-1981 - Associate Director of the Center for Radiophysics and Space Research (CRSR) at Cornell
  • 1972 - Created the first physical message, a golden plaque, sent into space on the Pioneer 10 space probe
  • 1976-1996 David Duncan Professor of Astronomy and Space Sciences
  • 1977 - Devised the Voyager Golden Record attached to the Voyager space probes
  • 1977 - Presented the Royal Institute Christmas Lectures in London
  • 1977 - Pulitser Prize for the book, Dragons of Eden
  • 1980 - Co-wrote and hosted the popular science documentary series Cosmos on PBS.
  • 1980 - Co-founded The Planetary Society, a civilian sponsored space advocacy organization
  • 1980-1996 - Dr. Sagan continued to support scientific inquiry especially in Space science. He continued to write about science and earned numerous awards nationally and internationally.
  • 1996 - Dr. Sagan died from complications of pneumonia at the age of 62.
